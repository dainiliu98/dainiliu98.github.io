Skin Deep

Macrophotography

Spring, 2019 | Category: Photography | Canon 6D MarkII

This macrophotography project started with my fun exploration of reversing my 24-135mm lens, which made ordinary things sensational (and gross?), like under a microscope.

This series I focused on my own body, documenting the reality of a female body. People’s first reactions to these photos are usually “Ewww! What are these?” I intended to make people uncomfortable and question what they are looking at. They represent the idea that under close examination, anything/person that appear to be beautiful will show flaws but at the same time may also reveal a different kind of beauty. This is likely to be the inspiration for my art senior show.

I also photographed close-ups of various makeup products and my face before-and-after-makeup comparisons because I was thinking about making a case for body image: how I was always self-conscious about concealing the blemishes of my skins and other features, but under macrophotography, the skin before and after makeups all look equally “gross” (or “beautiful,” depending on your perspective). I would like to name these photos “Skin Deep” to achieve irony.

Skin magazine is a slang for pornographic magazine, and I feel my photos could be pornographic if they were zoomed out and put in a different context. So another irony that interests me is that when the intimate parts of my body are blown up very big, suddenly they became less erotic
